I am a technologist, worker organizer, and public health researcher with a deep passion for ethics. Welcome to my page!

I spent the early stages of my career developing mobile applications that place the power of personal health back into the hands of the individual. There, I found a home in anti-military organizing within the tech industry, applying labor organizing principles to the fight for technology which uplifts rather than harms communities. Now I am completing an M.P.H. and M.S. in Bioethics at Columbia University, where I am exploring the questions which arise at the intersection of health, technology, and community advocacy.

Some of my work in this space include:

  • Psychosocial Interventions for Treatment Adherence among People Living with HIV/AIDS, presented at the NIH Clinical Center
  • Examining Bias in Machine Learning Tools for the Diagnosis of Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder
  • Labor organizing with No Tech For Apartheid, a coalition of tech workers fighting against Google and Amazon's involvement in defense contracting, weapons development, and the ongoing occupation of Palestine
  • Developing and piloting the Data Justice Think Tank, a learning program delivered in Spring 2025 to students at Columbia Mailman School of Public Health
  • Research at S.T.O.P, where I co-authored Dragnet City: NYPD's Omnipresent Domain Awareness System
  • Presentations on tech worker organizing strategy and principles at Take Back Tech 2024 and Circuit Breakers 2024

In my off hours I am also a ceramicist, painter, and avid reader. Most weekends you can find me playing volleyball with friends or exploring one of the many parks of New York City.
