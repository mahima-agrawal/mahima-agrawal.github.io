I am a public health technologist with a deep passion for ethics. Welcome to my page!

As a software engineer by trade I have spent my career developing mobile applications that place the power of personal health decisions back into the hands of the individual. Now I am completing an M.P.H. and M.S. in Bioethics at Columbia University, where I aim to explore the questions which arise at the intersection of health and technology.

Some of my work in this space include:

  • Psychosocial Interventions for Treatment Adherence among People Living with HIV/AIDS
  • Health Helper Mobile App: Game-ify the Experience of Taking Medications
  • Examining Bias in Machine Learning Tools for the Diagnosis of Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder
  • Labor organizing with No Tech For Apartheid, a coalition of tech workers fighting against Google and Amazon's involvement in defense contracting, weapons development, and the ongoing occupation of Palestine

In my off hours I am also a ceramicist, painter, and avid reader. Most weekends you can find me playing volleyball with friends or exploring one of the many parks of New York City.
